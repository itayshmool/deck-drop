$ describe --level 1

L1 MOST ORGS
Name
Personal Productivity
Signal
Individuals use AI. The org doesn't.
Desc

Individuals adopt AI independently — drafting, summarizing, coding. Gains are real but isolated. No shared tooling, no process change. Each person's AI usage is invisible to the system.

Diagnostic
"Is AI usage an individual habit or an organizational capability?"
Markers
Engineers use Copilot. PMs use ChatGPT. Designers use Midjourney. None of it coordinated or measured.
The tell
If one person leaves, their AI workflows leave with them. Nothing documented, shared, or institutionalized.
The trap
This is where most companies claiming "AI-forward" sit. Individual tool use masquerades as transformation.

L2

Team Workflow
Signal
Teams share AI processes — within their walls.
Desc

Teams have shared AI tools and processes within functional boundaries. Engineering has its pipeline, marketing has its workflow. Real gains — but workflows don't cross teams.

Diagnostic
"Can an AI workflow in one team trigger or feed a workflow in another?"
Markers
Standardized AI toolchains within teams. Shared prompts, templates, pipelines. Team-level metrics improve.
The tell
Cross-team handoffs are still manual. Data flows through meetings, tickets, docs — not integrated AI systems.
The wall
L2→L3 is the hardest jump. Requires shared data models, integrated systems of record, cross-team trust in AI actions.

$ describe --level 4
Level
L4
Name
Compounding Operating System
Signal
AI learns from itself. The org gets smarter automatically.
Desc

AI continuously learns and improves from past runs. Non-engineers ship production tools. Hierarchy flattens. Each cycle makes the next faster — compounding advantage, not linear.

Diagnostic
"Is your AI getting better on its own — without engineers manually improving it each time?"
Markers
Non-engineers ship production-grade tools. AI systems improve from accumulated data and feedback loops.
The tell
Last quarter's AI output is measurably worse than this quarter's — not from new features, but from self-learning.
The moat
Competitive advantage becomes structural. Competitors can copy your tools but not your accumulated learning.
$ describe --level 5
Level
L5
Name
Virtually Self-Driving
Signal
Humans govern. The system operates.
Desc

Core operating loops run autonomously — sensing, diagnosing, acting, learning. Humans set strategy, define boundaries, handle exceptions. The system does the rest. No org fully operates here today.

Diagnostic
"Could the core business run for a week with only strategic oversight and exception handling?"
Markers
Autonomous sense-diagnose-act-learn loops. Humans intervene by exception, not by default.
The tell
Organization responds to market shifts faster than any human decision chain could. Adaptation is continuous, not quarterly.
Reality
No organization is fully here. This is the north star — the direction, not the destination.
